# Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Artifact | A working or partially working tool, script, dashboard, GPT, agent, automation, workflow, app, or integration. |
| Prototype commons | The informal space where employee-built artifacts exist before formal classification. |
| Production class ladder | A classification model that separates personal tools, team betas, supported internal products, and customer-facing promises. |
| Reliance | The degree to which a person, team, process, customer, or business outcome depends on the artifact. |
| Hidden dependency | A tool or workflow the business depends on without formal ownership, controls, or support. |
| Promotion | Moving an artifact to a higher class with stronger ownership and controls. |
| Demotion | Moving an artifact to a lower class because evidence, controls, or reliance do not justify support. |
| Retirement | Intentionally removing or replacing an artifact. |
| Formalization | Routing the artifact into business case, charter, portfolio scoring, or governance operations because it needs investment or durable operating support. |
| Customer-facing promise | Any artifact whose behavior affects external users, customers, contractual commitments, trust, revenue, or brand. |
