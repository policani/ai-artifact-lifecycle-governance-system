# Promotion and Demotion Decision Rules

## Promote when

- The artifact solves a repeated, material workflow problem.
- Users demonstrate real pull or repeated usage.
- Data and system boundaries are understood.
- Ownership is clear or can be assigned.
- Failure modes are understood.
- Required controls are feasible.
- The benefit justifies support obligations.
- Support, maintenance, training, and exception-handling obligations are proportionate to the value signal.

## Hold as team beta when

- The workflow signal is promising but evidence is not yet strong.
- Risk is bounded.
- Users understand it is not a supported product.
- The owner can run a short proof cycle.

## Keep personal when

- Only one person uses it.
- It does not touch sensitive data or critical systems.
- It creates no hidden dependency.
- The organization should not spend governance energy on it.

## Demote when

- The artifact has grown beyond its evidence.
- Current use exceeds the controls in place.
- It should be narrowed back to personal or team-beta status.
- A better supported system exists.

## Retire when

- Value is weak.
- Risk is disproportionate.
- No owner exists.
- The tool duplicates a supported capability without a compelling reason.
- The support burden is not justified.
- It creates customer, data, operational, or trust exposure that cannot be controlled.

## Formalize when

- New funding, staffing, architecture, procurement, policy, or delivery commitment is needed.
- Route to business case, charter, portfolio scoring, or governance log as appropriate.

## Support-burden rule

Promotion should become harder as reliance and support obligation increase. A
personal tool may need only bounded risk and useful local value. A team beta
needs owner readiness and a short proof cycle. A promoted or formalized artifact
needs clearer value evidence, support owner, maintenance path, controls,
training expectations, failure handling, and downstream route.
